Organization Background

Himalayan Initiatives for Sustainable and Resilient Society (Himal-I) is a non-governmental and non-profit making organization established in 2020 A.D (2077 B.S.). It is registered in District Administration Office (Reg. No. 237) of Mustang district and affiliated in Social Welfare Council, Lalitpur (Affiliation No. 52069). We are devoted to enhance ecosystem and resilience of vulnerable community.

The head office of Himal-I is in Gharapjhong rural municipality of Mustang and Program Coordination Office in Kathmandu. The organization board consist of 9 members; representing 6 females and 3 males from indigenous community of Mustang district. For now, we have 3 staffs with academic background and experiences on forestry and watershed management (Mr. Nagendra Prakash Regmi); climate change/DRR and livelihood (Mr. Manjil Sherchan) and Chart of Accountant (Ms. Nishma Bhattachan). There are also advisors for the cross-cutting issues on GESI (Ms. Yasso Kanti hattachan), MEAL Expert (Mr. Sanoj Tulachan). For the quality project implementation and common understanding in a team, it has its own financial policy, procurement policy, HR policy, complaint handling policy and code of conduct.

Advisor

Yasso Kanti BhattachanGESI Expert

Yasso is a Thakali Indigenous women leader, activist and scholar whose ancestral land is in Thasang, Mustang district of Nepal. Yasso has dedicated her career to bring visibility and legal recognition to the rights of Indigenous women and girls in Nepal. She brings years of experience advocating for Indigenous women’s rights and climate justice at the local, national and international levels. She is one of the founding member and Advisor of the National Indigenous Women’s Federation, Regional Council Member of Asia Pacific Forum on Women, Law and Development (APWLD). At national forum, she has been playing a critical role in advocating for the full recognition and inclusion of Indigenous women and girls in the Nepal Constitution. Yasso contributes to promote and preserve indigenous practices that protect Mother Earth from anthropogenic activities that boost up climate change.

Sanoj Tulachan MEAL Expert

Tulachan holds MA in Conflict, Governance and International Development Studies from University of East Anglia, UK. He has worked as an MEAL expert since 2003 in different international organizations like Water Aid Nepal, Plan International, Helen Killer International and Concern World Wide. He has transferred his expertise in strengthening MEAL system of organization and the project he engaged. Similarly, he has published several knowledge management documents that indicates the progress of the project and as well as of organization goal and objectives.

Laxmi Devi MaharjanHydrologist

Ms. Maharjan holds MSc in Water Science and Engineering and as well as in Geology from International Institute of Hydraulics and Environmental Engineering Delft, Netherland. She has worked with national government agencies like National Reconstruction Authority (NRA)-Nepal, President Chure Terai Madhesh Conservation Board for mapping of flood and landslide risk through modelling. She has studied and produced research report on hydraulic modelling of more than 15 river basins of Nepal through support of local municipalities.
