COVID Response Project

After several months of relatively low daily cases in Nepal, COVID-19 cases began to rapidly spike in mid-April 2021, increasing consistently from 152 on 1 April to over 8,000 per day since 5 May 2021. With this, COVID 19 active cases were observed significantly high in compare to first COVID in Mustang district. Due to insufficient basic facilities in health units of Mustang district, the local communities were forced to travel Pokhara (158 Km) for health services such as COVID 19 test and oxygen refill which are the most important health equipment required for the patient suffering from COVID 19. Poor communities have similar symptoms but couldnot afford to travel Pokhara due to high travel cost because of lockdown..

Objective:

To minimize the spread of COVID 19 risk and provide quality health services to COVID affected patients through enhancing local health institutions.

Output 1:

Minimize the spread of COVID 19 risk providing preventive equipment’s to vulnerable community and front liner

  1. Provided COVID 19 preventive equipments to 580 vulnerable HHs of Barahgaun, Gharapjhong and Thasang Rural Municipalities of Mustang district.
  2. Supported COVID 19 preventive equipments to frontliners; Traffic police and Journalist.

Output 2:

Provide COVID 19 health equipments to health institutions

  1. Supported 250 antigen kits and 1 oxygen concentrator to Gharapjhong RM.
  2. Supported 15 oximeter to Lo-Ghekar Damodar Kunda RM.
  3. Supported 15 oximeter and 1 oxygen concentrator to Lomangthang RM
  4. Supported 10 oximeter and 1 oxygen concentrator to Thasang RM
